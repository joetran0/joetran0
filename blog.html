Teaching CV Courses Projects Winter 2025 Blog About Me

Blog

Sunday, April 13, 2025

I’m feeling extremely lost, upset, and disappointed with myself. I’m currently doing my Master’s in Pure Mathematics, and I wish I could say that it’s going well—but the truth is, it hasn’t been. I’ve been overwhelmed by how many hours I’ve spent studying, trying to prepare for exams, but I find myself unable to reproduce that work under pressure. And that disconnect is devastating.

I’ve been trying so hard to do well—especially on exams—that it has completely drained me, mentally and emotionally. And seeing the grade I received just added to that exhaustion. Every day, I wake up, go to school, study, come home, sleep, and repeat. That cycle hasn’t stopped for years. It’s like I’ve been running on autopilot. I’m not living—I’m just surviving.

This pattern keeps repeating: I do well enough on assignments, but I fall apart on exams. I used to think that was just a performance issue, but I now realize it may be more than that. I’ve been working myself to the bone, and the result has been full-on burnout. I feel like I’ve been mentally exhausted for the past five years. I keep telling myself I need a vacation, but I never give myself permission to take one. My mind doesn’t stop—it’s always full of math, even when I don’t want it to be.

Despite everything, I still want to complete my Master’s program. I still want to move forward with my PhD, if I can pass the courses that I struggled with. But more than anything, I want to protect my mental health—I want to be well enough to actually learn, to grow, and to live a life that isn’t consumed by this pressure. I want to find a version of this path that works for me, not one that breaks me.
